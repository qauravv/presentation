## 1. WHAT THIS IS

A 47-minute intellectual thriller disguised as a seminar. Not a lecture. Not a slideshow. A piece of theater that happens to use a screen.

## 5. VISUAL RULES â€” The Commandments

1. **No bullet points. Ever.** Line breaks, numbered sequences, visual cards, or spatial layout only. Bullets are the enemy of drama.
2. **Full-bleed images** where specified. Cinematic. AI-generated is encouraged for custom compositions. No generic stock. No clip art. No Shutterstock energy.
3. **Max 3 text elements** on any image slide. The image IS the slide; text is a whisper over it.
4. **Text-only slides = movie title cards.** Centered. Dramatic whitespace. Let the words breathe. If it doesn't feel like it could be a frame in a film, it has too much on it.
5. **Animations: fade-in on click ONLY.** No fly-ins, no bounces, no zooms, no spins. Every motion is a quiet arrival. Fade duration: 0.5â€“0.8 seconds.
6. **Dark slides (Obsidian) = emotional beats.** Light slides (Bone) = teaching moments. The alternation creates rhythm â€” inhale, exhale.
7. **Consistent caption placement:** Bottom-left for image sources/attributions. Always. Predictability lets the eye relax.
8. **The 3-Second Rule:** If someone can't absorb a slide's core idea in 3 seconds of looking, it has too much.
9. **Breathing room:** Minimum 40px margin on all sides. Text never crowds edges. Whitespace is a design element, not wasted space.
10. **Image treatment:** All photographs get a very subtle vignette (darkened edges, ~5%) to focus attention center. All illustrations/diagrams sit on clean backgrounds without borders unless specified.
